Who Were the Signers

Signers of the Declaration of Independence

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, known for his bold signature that became a symbol of resistance and confidence.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence, who articulated the coloniesâ€™ beliefs in natural rights and freedom.
Benjamin Franklin
A respected inventor and diplomat who helped unify the colonies and secure critical foreign support, especially from France.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence who helped persuade others to support separation from Britain and later became president.
Samuel Adams
A revolutionary leader who organized resistance against British rule and motivated public support for independence.
Roger Sherman
A key political leader and the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States.
Robert Morris
A financier who used his wealth and resources to help fund the Revolutionary War.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
A wealthy landowner and the only Catholic signer, who supported independence despite great personal risk.

The signers of the Declaration of Independence helped create the United States through their leadership, ideas, and courage. They promoted freedom, gained support, funded the war, and risked their lives by standing up to Britain to form a new nation.
